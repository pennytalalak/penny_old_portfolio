 Problem

After analysing the ABS statistic on NSW high school graduation rate by age and gender, the problem arose when there is a drastic gap between the North Sydney/CBD area and Western Sydney. Through the investigation of locating private school around NSW, it is evident that top 10 school in NSW are mostly above the "Latte Line". We believe that education should be equally disperse throughout NSW through better teaching and environment.

 Solution

Tri-Eskola: A Multifunctional Hub

 Architecture

Tri-Eskola is a multifunctional educational hub that facilitates both students, teachers and staffs. The hub contains a school that is selective for HSC students only, a teacher training centre and a boarding house for students.


With the benefit of locating circumferentially around the Whitlam Leisure Centre, both the public users, students and teachers are allowed to interact between an intensed academic environment and the recreational space.

Teacher Training Centre

Better education not only comes from the willingness to learn of the individual but also the quality of teaching being delivered. The Training Centre trains teachers to perform well in class as well as developing interpersonal skills for better relationships between students and teachers.


Teacher Training Centre comprises meeting rooms, a lecture theatre, a food court and workshop areas.

The Boarding House

The boading house facilitates students who face difficulties in travelling to and from home. This eliminates discrimination for students who travel long distances and allows them to experience quality education and welcoming facilities.


The boarding house comprises 2 towers, separately for males and females, a dining hall, a common room, and bathrooms.

Whitlam College

Whitlam College, named after the Whitlam Leisure Centre, offers quality HSC education for senior students. Taught by well-trained teachers, students are supported towards better results and prepared for the real world. As a result, high school graduate rates should dramatically increase in the next 40 years.


Whitlam College comprises of classrooms, specifically for HSC subjects, FABLAB workshop, a music room, meeting rooms, an assembly hall, a food court, and an in-house office.

The Whitlam Ring

The Whitlam Ring serves as a mean for travelling between each tower. Locating the journey on top instead of the ground prevents the deconstruction of land and forest surrounded. Whilst presenting a very open space for students as a recreational space, the ring holds a simple railed pod system to escort from one tower to another.


Ultimately, the ring has the ability to translate up and down on each towers, in order to create shade for the Whitlam Leisure Centre according to the sun's direction.

 Site Analysis

From "Directions for a Greater Sydney 2017-2056" by the Greater Sydney Commission, it is predicted that the next global metropolis of three productive, liveable and sustainable cities are Western Parkland City, Central River City and Eastern Harbour City.


Due to a high rise populated area in the Sydney City region, there is an opportunity for the Parramatta area and Western Sydney to become the next CBD with the airport being currently in progress.


It is evident that investment in education will be shifting towards Western Sydney, however there is still a significant gap in the South West area.

 Site Plan

Liverpool has been chosen as a locating site for Tri-Eskola. This is due to Liverpool being proximity to the Greater Parramatta, Western Sydney and 50 mins train to Sydney City. The available space around The Whitlam Leisure Centre is suitable for locating Tri-Eskola where people can retreat, recreate and relax after intensed education.
